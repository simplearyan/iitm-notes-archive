**A matrix is in row echelon form if :  
  
**

‚ÄĘ The first non-zero element (the leading entry) in a row is 1.

‚ÄĘ The column containing the leading 1 of a row is to the right of the column containing the leading 1 of the row above it.

‚ÄĘ Any non-zero rows are always above rows with all zeros.  
  

A matrix is in reduced row echelon form if :  
  

‚ÄĘ The first non-zero element in the first row (the leading entry) is the number 1.

‚ÄĘ All subsequent non-zero rows must also have their leading entries (i.e. first non-zero entries) as 1 and they should appear to the right of the leading entry in the previous row.

‚ÄĘ The leading entry in each row must be the only non-zero number in its column.

‚ÄĘ Any non-zero rows are always above rows with all zeros.  
  

Note: Any matrix which is in reduced row echelon form is also in row echelon form.

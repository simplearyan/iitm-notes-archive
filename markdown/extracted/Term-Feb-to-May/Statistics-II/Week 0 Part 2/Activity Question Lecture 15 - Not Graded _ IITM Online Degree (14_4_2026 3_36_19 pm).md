Spins will be independent to each other.

A circular board is divided into five equal sections, numbered from $1$ to $5$. A spinning arrow on the board is equally likely to land on any number.

 If the arrow is spinned four times, then what is the probability that number $3$ will not appear in any of the spins?
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
###  No, the answer is incorrect. 
Score: 0

### Feedback:
Let $A$ be the event of getting any number other than three on any spin. 
 Then, number of outcomes in $A = 4$ (all numbers other than $3$)
  $P(A)$ = (no of elements in $A$) / (no of elements in $S$)

            = $4 / 5$

 $P$(number $3$ will not appear in spinning the spinner four times)= $(4 / 5)^{4}$ 
(Since all spins are independent to each other)
